# About Text Analysis International

The mission of Text Analysis International (TAI) was to revolutionize the way in which people process text.

TAI was founded in 1998 to develop technologies to analyze text deeply, accurately, and extensibly. VisualText embodied that technological approach, enabling a Natural Language Engineering (NLE) methodology for developing text analyzers. With NLE, the user controlled the order of execution, the contexts to be worked in, and the actions to be taken in every step of the analysis of a text.

TAI's goals included an ever-greater degree of automation in the analysis of text, but always with a pragmatic, rather than theoretical, foundation. TAI developed standardized bodies of knowledge, methods for text analysis, and diverse applications as it broadened its core technology. Rather than championing any particular paradigm, TAI sought to exploit all paradigms and methods and to integrate methods wherever useful. Rather than over-committing to keyword, statistical, neural network, pattern, recursive grammar, or other approaches, TAI sought to encompass all these approaches and more within its product sets.

Text Analysis International was shuttered in December 2018. A new company, [Conceptual Systems](https://conceptualsystems.biz/), was subsequently formed by NLP++ co-author Amnon Meyers to carry the technology forward.
